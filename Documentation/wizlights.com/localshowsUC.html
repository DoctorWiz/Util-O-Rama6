Other Awesome Christmas Light Displays on the First Coast - Greater Jacksonville Area

Westside:

Christmas on Crystal Springs

10580 Crystal Springs Road, Jacksonville  - Off of Chaffee Rd. Near I-10.

This is probably the biggest single animated & musical light show in the Northeast Florida area.  Winner of ABC's "Great Christmas Light Fight" in 2014!  Over 90,000 lights on thousands of channels.  M.C.ed by a giant robotic snowman.  I have personally visited this display and it is awesome.  There is a lot of traffic.  For best viewing, park in the empty lot just west of the display and walk down the sidewalk and watch for a while!

East Arlington:

Blackhawk Bluff Subdivision on Girvin Road.

I have personally visited this display, and can't wait to go again!  Despite the absolutely horrible traffic, this is a must see!  Nearly every house in these 2 neighborhoods is decorated.  It's an older neighborhood with giant old oak trees many of them decorated with giant light up stars and ornaments.  Many residents pass out candy canes, hot chocolate, and cookies.



12055 Evans Bluff Court, Jacksonville.  - Near Beach & Kernan.

About 20,000 LED lights, lots of hanging stars, snowflakes, lighted balls and meteor lights hanging across the street. Another on my list for this year.  The pictures look great!





West Arlington:

4568 Royal Port Drive, Jacksonville.  - N. end of University, past J.U.

Over 16,000 lights set to music!  A 30 minute show featuring 11 songs!  I have personally visited this show, and while not the biggest and the brightest, it is still quite good and I recommend it!





North Mandarin:

3710 Beauclerc Road, Jacksonville.

I visited this one twice last year. A huge walk through static light show (not animated or musical), another one of the biggest in the area. The owner has being doing this for many years and the display is well known, so traffic can sometimes be a problem.

St. Johns County:

Synchronized Christmas light display using 64 channels of Light-o-Rama controllers. Approx. 5,000 lights, 27 songs. Display starts the weekend after Thanksgiving and runs through New Year's Eve. Complete show runs about 90 minutes and runs from 5:30PM - Midnight.  I visited this one last year, and it is very well done!

Tune your radio to 103.9 FM.

Conklin's Mack 5 light show
4635 Petunia Avenue,  - Take Blanding thru M’burg past 218 to Peppergrass St. (1st right after 218, .7 mi., Go 1.2 mi to Calendula Cir, turn Left, .3 mi to Petunia on R, house on L. at corner.
This one is a bit tricky to find. This light show is connected to Light O Rama so you get the beauty of the lights along with the music and dancing. They are on the whimsical side so lots of fun for the children. They have around 50,000 lights give or take. They have a 20ft mega tree, blow mold, deer, snowman and much much more.  I visited this show last year.  It's out in the boonies.  It's a nice display, but I don't think it's worth the drive unless you are already nearby.

1689 Long Horn Road, Middleburg.  - Take Blanding thru M’burg to 218 West. Approx 7 miles to Long Horn Rd, turn right. 1/3 mile on right.
Approximately 40,000 lights dancing to music. I also visited this one last year, and again, it's out in the boonies.  Another nice display, but I'm not sure this one is worth a long drive either, but definately recommended if you live nearby.
